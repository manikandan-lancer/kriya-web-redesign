Product Overview

Spindura Rix is a premium suspension concentrate formulation containing 45% Spinosad, a highly effective insecticide derived from the soil bacterium Saccharopolyspora spinosa. This advanced formulation combines the exceptional pest control capabilities of Spinosad with enhanced stability and extended residual activity.

Spindura Rix provides rapid knockdown and long-lasting control of lepidopteran pests and other targeted insects while maintaining excellent environmental safety profiles. The Rix formulation features improved rainfastness, better plant coverage, and enhanced compatibility with integrated pest management programs.

Recommended Dosage

Crop Type Dosage per Acre Water Volume
Cotton 80-100 ml / acre 200-300 liters water
Vegetables 60-80 ml / acre 200-400 liters water
Fruits 80-120 ml / acre 400-600 liters water
Pulses 60-80 ml / acre 200-300 liters water
Cereals 80-100 ml / acre 200-300 liters water

Application Guidelines: Shake well before use. Prepare suspension in clean water and use within 4-6 hours. Apply during early morning or late evening hours. Ensure thorough coverage of both leaf surfaces. Repeat applications at 10-14 day intervals based on pest pressure. Avoid application during peak sunlight hours or when bees are actively foraging.

Mode of Action

Spindura Rix employs a unique biological mode of action that makes it highly effective against target pests while maintaining environmental safety:

Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptor Activation: Spinosad acts on the nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the insect nervous system, causing rapid excitation of nerve cells.

GABA Receptor Interaction: Simultaneously affects GABA-gated chloride channels, leading to disrupted nerve function and uncontrolled muscle contractions.

Rapid Paralysis: The combined action on multiple neural targets results in rapid paralysis and feeding cessation within hours of exposure.

Ingestion and Contact Activity: Works primarily through ingestion but also has significant contact activity, providing multiple routes of entry.

Selective Toxicity: The unique mode of action provides selective toxicity to target pests while being relatively safe to most beneficial insects, mammals, and birds.

Minimal Resistance Risk: The complex mode of action reduces the likelihood of resistance development compared to conventional insecticides.
