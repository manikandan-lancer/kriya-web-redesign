MYCOVAEC

Beauveria bassiana Bioinsecticide

Advanced entomopathogenic fungus for sustainable pest management

Beauveria bassiana 1.15% WP Broad-spectrum Eco-friendly IPM compatible

Product Overview

Mycova EC is a highly effective emulsifiable concentrate formulation containing Beauveria bassiana, a naturally occurring entomopathogenic fungus. This advanced biological insecticide targets a wide range of insect pests through a unique infection process that is safe for humans, animals, and beneficial insects.

Mycova EC provides sustainable pest control by infecting target insects through contact, leading to mortality within 3-7 days. The EC formulation ensures excellent coverage, adhesion, and fungal viability for maximum efficacy in various agricultural and horticultural applications.

Key Features

Biological Control

Uses naturally occurring fungus for eco-friendly pest management without chemical residues.

Broad Spectrum

Effective against multiple insect pests including whiteflies, thrips, aphids, and mites.

Residual Activity

Provides extended protection with fungal spores remaining active for 7-14 days.

Selective Action

Targets specific pest species while being safe for pollinators and beneficial insects.

Resistance Management

Unique mode of action helps prevent resistance development in pest populations.

Recommended Dosage

Crop Type Dosage per Acre Water Volume Application Interval
Vegetables 500-750 ml / acre 200-400 liters 7-10 days
Fruits 750-1000 ml / acre 400-600 liters 10-14 days
Cotton 500-750 ml / acre 200-300 liters 7-10 days
Pulses 400-600 ml / acre 200-300 liters 10-14 days
Cereals 400-600 ml / acre 200-300 liters 10-14 days

Application Guidelines: Shake well before use. Mix required quantity in small amount of water first, then add to spray tank. Apply during early morning or late evening hours. Avoid application during hot, dry conditions. Maintain adequate humidity for optimal fungal activity. Do not mix with chemical fungicides. Repeat applications based on pest pressure and crop growth stage.

Mode of Action

Mycova EC employs a unique biological mode of action through the entomopathogenic fungus Beauveria bassiana:

Contact and Adhesion: Fungal spores adhere to the insect cuticle upon contact with the target pest.

Germination and Penetration: Spores germinate and produce enzymes that dissolve the insect's cuticle, allowing fungal hyphae to penetrate into the body cavity.

Colonization: The fungus grows inside the insect, utilizing nutrients and producing toxins that eventually kill the host.

Sporulation: After the insect dies, the fungus grows out through the cuticle and produces new spores that can infect other insects.

Secondary Spread: Infected insects can spread the disease to other pests in the population through contact.

Environmental Safety: The fungus is specific to insects and does not affect mammals, birds, or beneficial organisms.
